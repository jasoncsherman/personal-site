When you publish something half-formed, you trade the comfort of privacy for the pressure of an audience. That pressure is the point: it forces clarity you'd otherwise postpone.

A few things I'm noticing as I start to write more openly:

- The fear of being wrong in public shrinks the more you do it.
- Half-finished thoughts attract better feedback than polished ones.
- Writing to be read is a different muscle than writing to think.
